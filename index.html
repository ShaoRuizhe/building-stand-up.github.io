OffNadir Satellite Images -> Building 3D Mesh Urban 3D mesh models are one of the most intuitive and widely used forms for understanding cities. However, the current mainstream 3D mesh reconstruction methods face the challenges of complex inputs, intricate reconstruction processes, and high computational costs. To address this issue, this paper proposes a novel pipeline for reconstructing urban building 3D meshes based on a single off-nadir satellite remote sensing image. This pipeline relies on a multitask neural network to extract building polygons and height information from the image. Subsequently, using this information, a 3D mesh model is constructed. We further propose a multitask neural network named ONBPNet (Off-Nadir Building Polygon Network) for our pipeline. This neural network introduces our proposed cross-field auxiliary task and multi-scale mask head to extract building polygons with more accurate shapes. Through extensive experiments, we demonstrate that our pipeline can automatically and rapidly construct easily observable 3D models of urban buildings. Our ONBPNet outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in extracting precise building polygons, thereby enhancing the accuracy of the final 3D mesh generated by the pipeline.
